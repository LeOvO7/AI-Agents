Question: How do I adjust the carburetor on a Model T?
----------------------------------------
Adjusting the carburetor on a Model T Ford involves a few steps to ensure proper fuel-air mixture and engine performance. Here’s a general guide to help you with the adjustment:

1. **Warm Up the Engine**: Start the engine and let it warm up to operating temperature. This helps in making accurate adjustments.

2. **Locate the Carburetor**: The Model T typically has a Holley or Kingston carburetor. Familiarize yourself with its components, including the mixture adjustment screw and the throttle.

3. **Adjust the Mixture**: 
   - Locate the mixture adjustment screw, usually found on the side of the carburetor.
   - Start with the screw turned in (clockwise) until it is lightly seated, then back it out (counterclockwise) about 1.5 to 2 turns. This is a good starting point.
   - While the engine is running, slowly turn the screw in or out to find the point where the engine runs smoothly. If the engine stumbles or runs rough, adjust the screw slightly until you achieve a smooth idle.

4. **Check the Throttle**: 
   - Ensure that the throttle is functioning properly. The throttle lever should move freely and return to the closed position when released.
   - Adjust the throttle if necessary, ensuring it opens and closes fully.

5. **Test Drive**: After making adjustments, take the Model T for a short drive. Pay attention to how it accelerates and idles. If it hesitates or stalls, you may need to readjust the mixture.

6. **Fine-Tuning**: Repeat the mixture adjustment process as needed until you achieve optimal performance. 

7. **Final Check**: Once satisfied with the adjustments, ensure all components are secure and there are no fuel leaks.

Remember, adjustments may vary based on the specific carburetor model and engine condition, so it may take some trial and error to get it just right. Always refer to the owner's manual or a service guide for specific instructions related to your Model T.

Question: How do I fix a slipping transmission band?
----------------------------------------
Fixing a slipping transmission band typically involves several steps, and it's important to note that this can be a complex task that may require professional assistance. Here are some general steps you can take to address the issue:

1. **Check Transmission Fluid**: Low or dirty transmission fluid can cause slipping. Check the fluid level and condition. If it's low, top it off with the appropriate type of fluid. If it's dirty or burnt, consider changing it.

2. **Inspect for Leaks**: Look for any signs of fluid leaks around the transmission. If you find any, you may need to repair the seals or gaskets.

3. **Adjust the Band**: Some transmissions have adjustable bands. If your vehicle's transmission allows for this, you can adjust the band tension according to the manufacturer's specifications. This usually involves locating the band adjustment screw and tightening it slightly.

4. **Replace the Band**: If the band is worn or damaged, it may need to be replaced. This typically requires disassembling the transmission, which can be a complicated process. If you're not experienced with transmission work, it's advisable to seek professional help.

5. **Check for Other Issues**: Slipping can also be caused by other transmission problems, such as a faulty torque converter, worn clutches, or issues with the valve body. A thorough diagnostic may be necessary to identify the root cause.

6. **Consult a Professional**: If you're unsure about any of the steps or if the problem persists after your attempts to fix it, it's best to consult a qualified mechanic or transmission specialist. They can provide a more accurate diagnosis and recommend the appropriate repairs.

Always refer to your vehicle's service manual for specific instructions and specifications related to your transmission.
